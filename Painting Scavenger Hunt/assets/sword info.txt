Edged weapons were not uncommon during the civil war, though they were mostly found on officers, naval units, cavalry, and artillery. Given the location being a fort, it is likely that the man sleeping nearby the sabre is a sergeant. While swords are not a sufficient defense when dealing with firing arms, they are obviously extremely effective in close combat.  They are an extension of your arm, and can defend the swordsman from attacks as well as provide a quick defeat over a nearby enemy.
